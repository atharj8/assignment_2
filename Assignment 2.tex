\flushleft{\textbf{Question 1:}}
Examine the consistency of the system of given equation\\\\
\vspace{5}
\textbf{(a)} $$x+2y=2$$
$$2x+3y=3 $$ \\
\textbf{Sol:}\\
\vspace{5}
In matrix form, this can be written as \textbf{AX=B}\\
\vspace{5}
where A = \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2\\
2 & 3\\
\end{bmatrix}, X = \begin{bmatrix}
x\\
y\\
\end{bmatrix} and B = \begin{bmatrix}
2\\
3\\
\end{bmatrix}\\
\vspace{5}
Now if the \textbf{rank of matrix A = rank of augmented matrix (A\vert \textbf{B)}},\\ 
\vspace{5}
then the system of equation is consistent otherwise inconsistent.\\
\vspace{5}
Forming Augmented matrix (A\vert B)=\begin{bmatrix}
1&2&\aug & 2\\
2&3&\aug &3\\
\end{bmatrix}\\
\vspace{5}
Now , In order to find the rank, we use row operations.\\
$$(A\vert B)=\begin{bmatrix}
1&2&\aug & 2\\
2&3&\aug &3\\
\end{bmatrix}\\$$\\
R2 = R2 - 2R1\\
$$(A\vert B)=\begin{bmatrix}
1&2&\aug & 2\\
0&-1&\aug &-1\\
\end{bmatrix}\\$$\\
As rank of matrix A = rank of Augmented matrix (A\vert B) = n(no. of unknowns)\\
\vspace{5}
Hence the system is consistent and has unique solutions.\\
\vspace{5}
Now in order to find the solutions, we use the \textbf{Guass Jordan \vspace{5} Elimination method}.
For this, We again use row operations.\\
\paragraph.
R2 = (-1)R2\\
$$(A\vert B)=\begin{bmatrix}
1&2&\aug & 2\\
0&1&\aug &1\\
\end{bmatrix}\\$$\\
R1 = R1 - 2R2\\
$$(A\vert B)=\begin{bmatrix}
1&0&\aug & 0\\
0&1&\aug &1\\
\end{bmatrix}\\$$\\
From above matrix, it  is clear that value of \textbf{x=0} and value of \textbf{y=1.}\\
